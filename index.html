Drawing inspiration from the game Journey By Thatgamecompany and Nintendo's The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom in both visual style (cartoonish 3D) and gameplay mechanics, Distant Wind is as much a game that heavily emphasises movement and environment traversal as it is a piece of artistic media.
Although this pitch is in a top-down 2D format, the general 'feel' as well as collectible-driven nature very much parallels our intentions for Distant Wind. Not only does this freedom of exploration and travel allow for a unique approach to storytelling, but also heavily backs the aim of the story - to portray human life from an objective perspective.

This artistic aspect of the game is punctuated by its visual style and dynamic soundscape - where the music morphs to the current 'intensity' of the situation, and players feel as if they are traversing an everchanging landscape painting.

The aim of this gameplay upgrade driven story is to create a replayable game that encourages players to progress the story, not by the implementation of hard limits - rather making the game more enjoyable.
To summarise our plans for Distant Wind, we want to create a satisfying to play movement-based adventure/exploration game set in a beautiful dynamic environment with semi-cartoonish 3D graphics based around a fun, cute, and heartening story about life.
